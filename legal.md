        <h1>Popcorn Hack</h1>
        
        <h2>Define a Creative Commons License</h2>
        <p>A <strong>Creative Commons (CC) License</strong> allows creators to share their work with some rights reserved, making it easier for others to reuse while maintaining attribution.</p>
        
        <h2>What are exceptions to copyright law?</h2>
        <p>Some uses of copyrighted material are legally exempt, including:</p>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Fair Use</strong> – Using copyrighted material for education, commentary, or research.</li>
            <li><strong>Public Domain</strong> – Works with expired copyrights or explicitly waived rights.</li>
            <li><strong>Creative Commons</strong> – Licensed content allowing free use with certain restrictions.</li>
        </ul>
        
        <h2>Legal and Ethical Concerns</h2>
        <p>Computing innovations can have unintended ethical and legal consequences:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Illegal streaming violates copyright laws and can distribute malware.</li>
            <li>Biased algorithms can misrepresent or exclude certain groups.</li>
            <li>Data tracking raises privacy concerns (e.g., step-tracking apps).</li>
            <li>The digital divide affects access to technology.</li>
            <li>Misinformation spreads rapidly on the internet.</li>
            <li>Social media algorithms contribute to political polarization.</li>
            <li>AI-generated art raises concerns about originality and theft.</li>
        </ul>
